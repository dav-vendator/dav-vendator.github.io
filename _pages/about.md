I am a PhD Research Scholar at IIT Bombay working at the intersection of Quantum Information Theory, Quantum Machine Learning, and sequential learning systems.

My work explores how quantum systems can represent memory, temporal correlations, and evolving structure through coherent dynamics.

Coming from a computer science and machine learning background, I am particularly interested in bridging ideas from recurrent neural networks, temporal representation learning, and information theory with quantum systems and quantum computation.


## Current Research

My current research focuses on coherent quantum recurrent architectures and the emergence of temporal memory in quantum systems.

A major direction of my work investigates whether entanglement and coherent evolution can provide effective representations for sequential dependencies while avoiding repeated destructive measurements during temporal processing.

Some themes I actively explore include:

- Quantum recurrent architectures
- Temporal correlations and memory
- Quantum Machine Learning
- Information-theoretic perspectives of learning
- Efficient representation of sequential systems
- Coherent evolution and deferred measurement


## Background

Before joining IIT Bombay, I completed my M.Tech at IIIT Delhi, where I worked on Quantum Machine Learning and proposed a coherent Quantum LSTM architecture designed to preserve quantum coherence across time steps.

My broader interests lie in understanding how ideas from learning, memory, correlations, and information emerge across both classical and quantum systems.
